Oil & gas
Salazar: Strongly disagrees with current Republican Attorney General Cynthia Coffman’s decision to sue Boulder County over its handling of oil and gas laws. Would move to withdraw the state’s Colorado Supreme Court appeal on the “Martinez case,” questioning whether oil and gas regulators need to weigh public health and the environment when issuing new drilling permits. Weiser: Agrees with Salazar
TABOR
Salazar: Says he would like to see Colorado’s Taxpayer’s Bill of Rights repealed. “I will use my discretion not to defend TABOR — to let it fall in the courts. Thatâ€™s the fastest way to get rid of it. If it is not gotten rid of there, I will be working with the legislature, as well as the people of the state of Colorado, to get them to understand that we need to repeal it.” Weiser: Says he would like to see TABOR reformed through a vote of the people. “I will work to change the law because TABOR is a straight jacket in our state and it's going to kill us if we don’t change it. Guns
Salazar: Has vowed to uphold and enforce Colorado’s 2013 gun control laws expanding background checks and banning high-capacity magazines. He is supportive of a so-called “red flag law,” a ban on bump stocks and a ban on assault weapons. Weiser: Has vowed to uphold and enforce Colorado’s 2013 gun control laws expanding background checks and banning high-capacity magazines. He is supportive of a so-called “red flag law,” a ban on bump stocks and a ban on assault weapons.
Marijuana
Salazar: Says he is very pro-cannabis and pro-hemp and would sit down with federal prosecutors in Colorado to “tell them to stay out of it.” Salazar agrees with the Obama-era Cole Memorandum, that dictates federal law enforcement will focus on marijuana being illegally shipped across state lines and not interfere with pot production and sales in states that have legalized it. Weiser: “The attorney general can fight commandeering by the federal government. ... I believe the way Obama handled it, under the Cole Memorandum, was the right way.”
Sanctuary cities
Salazar: “I will protect our undocumented families as well as our citizen families here in the state of Colorado from being terrorized by the Trump administration. If that means that I have to sue local governments as well as the state government that are trying to assist in federal immigration enforcement, I will do that.” Weiser: Says he supports and would support sanctuary cities. “They need to know they aren’t going to be deported when they show up in court,” he said of immigrants. “... The question here is what can an attorney general do? And the answer is defend the constitutional rights of cities to (be sanctuary cities).”
President Donald Trump
Salazar: “After Trump got elected, all the things I’ve done as a state legislator as well as a civil rights attorney — I thought this was the time, if we ever needed a street fighter, this is the time that we needed one in the office of attorney general.” Weiser: “It was the catalyzing reason. The legal term is it was the ‘but for’ cause. But for Trump getting elected I would not be running for attorney general.”
Death penalty
Salazar: “I find it to be morally repugnant and also unconstitutional, so I’m not so certain that I would defend the death penalty.” Weiser: Is against the death penalty for a host of reasons, but said: “I would absolutely defend the death penalty. That’s part of enforcement the law — if we have the death penalty I would carry out the role of the office.”
